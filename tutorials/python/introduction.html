Introduction

Over the years of learning python, I have found that a lot of courses for beginners are either too dry, or too difficult for users to understand. I think the main reason is that the pacing for many of the textbooks out there come from an information first approach. This means that they are motivated to give you all the things you need to know up front. This preloading is nice in that you learn alot, but without practice it is difficult to retain everything. Thus the approach that I want to use for this series of tutorials is a project based implementation. The idea is simple: learn as you go, and only learn what you need for the task at hand. Why do do this? Wont people leave without a clear understanding of all the python features? Yes! And that is ok! As I have embarked along my programming journey I have realized that the primary skill a programmer needs is google-fu - the ability to find answers to the problems that you have. Over time, after doing projects and reading, you will pick up the skills and knowledge you need. This means everything that you do will be useful and you can get up and running really quickly. This is especially important for the beginner who, in the beginning, needs to make steady progress to feel accomplished.

How To Go Through The Course

The course is split up into two parts. The first section is the Projects Section. This contains all the projects that will take you from zero to hero in python. Complete the projects to complete the course. The second section is the Reference Section. If you have difficulties knowing what to do or need a reference for somethig you forgot, the Reference Section is your friend. This section is meant to be the accompanied reading for the projects. The beauty is that you are not expected to read each chapter in totality, but rather look for the informatin that is the most relevant to the task at hand. On your first time looking at a chapter you will need to read most of it, but as you progress you may only need to check for specific topics to brush up on. Do your best to read through the Reference Section before you go to google. This might seem like a strange request being that programmers only need to know google-fu, but the readings are tailored specific to the projects, and will likely be easier to use. That being said, for the more difficult projects, or if you can't find the information that you are looking for, google away! In each project will be a list of relevant chapters to read that will both give help in completing the projects as well as hints as to how to complete a given task.
